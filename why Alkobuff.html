A study to evaluate the effect of Alkobuff buffer in rations with different acidogenic power
and to compare its performance with common buffering compounds was conducted
by the Industrial Relations Department of Ferdowsi University of Mashhad.
The general result of the experiment was as follows: Overall,
Alkobuff was found to effectively maintain the pH of rumen fluid in vitro.
However, unlike certain alkalizers, it did not reduce the disappearance of dry matter
(an indicator that may be related to the level of digestion in the digestive system of ruminants).


What is a buffer?
Buffers are defined as a combination
of a weak acid and a salt of that acid,
which helps maintain the pH in the rumen.


What is an alkalizer?
An alkalizer is a substance that neutralizes
acid and increases pH levels.

Overall, it can be concluded that Alkobuff is
effective in maintaining the pH of rumen fluid in vitro.
Moreover, unlike some types of alkalizers,
it does not significantly reduce the disappearance of dry matter—an
index related to the extent of digestion in the ruminant digestive system.
